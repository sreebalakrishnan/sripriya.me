Near Auroville, Pondicherry, India

I was born in Nagpur and grew up in a household where education was held sacred. It was in Bombay — where I moved for further studies — that I found my voice: my love for fashion, colour, beauty, and a deep curiosity about how we present ourselves to the world.

Yoga arrived early and never left. Two decades as a student taught me that the mat is not separate from life — it is life, distilled. Today I teach online and, when the moment calls for it, in person near Auroville — a place that mirrors everything I believe about conscious living.

Alongside yoga, I've spent years studying Vedic and tantric wisdom, volunteering with NGOs, and exploring image consulting — the art of helping people understand the language of what they wear. These aren't separate pursuits; they are one conversation about how we inhabit ourselves.

My family — my mother's quiet fierce resilience, my brother Harsh's steadfast love, my children, my closest friends — is the ground I stand on. I am grateful, always.

Whatever gentleness you find in me, I owe to my father. He gave me that — and I carry it as one of the most precious things I have.

Online yoga

Since the early days of COVID, I've been teaching online — and I've discovered that presence has nothing to do with being in the same room. Students across the world have made real, lasting changes to their wellbeing from their living rooms.

I offer personalised sessions for women navigating hormonal health — PCOD, PCOS, pregnancy, postpartum recovery, perimenopause, and menopause. The body knows how to heal; yoga simply clears the path.
