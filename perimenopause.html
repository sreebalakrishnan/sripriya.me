The Perimenopause Reset

A gentle, seven-day journey through the change — daily flows, breath work, nourishing food, simple lifestyle shifts and affirmations to help you move through perimenopause with strength, softness and ease. No experience needed. Just you, your breath, and a little space on the floor.

7 days 15–50 min a day All levels Yoga · Breath · Food · Rest
Why this course

A kinder way through the change

Perimenopause is not an illness — it is a passage. For a few years before your periods finally stop, your hormones rise and fall like tides, and your body, mood and sleep can feel like they belong to someone else. It can be confusing. It does not have to be lonely.

This course gathers everything I share with the women I teach — gentle movement to keep your bones and muscles strong, breath to cool the heat and calm the mind, food you can find in your own kitchen, and words to speak to yourself with tenderness. Seven short days to build a practice you can carry for the rest of this season of life.

You are not losing yourself. You are becoming more yourself.

How to use this course

  • Move through one day at a time, or repeat any day that serves you. There is no rush.
  • Wear soft clothes. A mat, a cushion and a wall are all you need; keep a block or folded blanket nearby.
  • Let the breath lead. If a pose strains, soften or skip it — sensation, never pain.
  • Read each day's affirmation aloud. Spoken daily, the mind begins to believe it.
  • Hydrate, and practise on a fairly empty stomach where you can.

Day One · Understand

Meeting the Change

Understand

Perimenopause is the natural transition leading up to menopause — the years when your ovaries gradually make less oestrogen. It often begins in your early-to-mid 40s (sometimes the late 30s) and lasts, on average, about four years — though for some it is a few months and for others closer to a decade.

You remain in perimenopause until you have gone a full twelve months without a period. That single milestone is menopause itself; every day after is post-menopause. So menopause is one day — perimenopause is the whole journey towards it.

Because oestrogen and progesterone rise and fall unevenly before they settle, symptoms come and go. Common signs: irregular or changing periods, hot flushes, night sweats, broken sleep, mood swings, anxiety or low mood, brain fog, fatigue, aching joints, drier skin, lower libido, and weight that gathers around the middle. As oestrogen falls it also affects bone density and heart health — which is exactly why movement and care matter now.

Yoga will not stop the tide, but it steadies the boat — calming the nervous system, easing stress hormones, supporting sleep and mood, keeping joints supple and bones strong, and gently reuniting you with a body that may feel unfamiliar.

Grounding practice≈ 20 min
  1. 1
    Easy seat & belly breath Sukhasana
    Sit tall, palms on belly. Breathe slow and low — 2 min.
  2. 2
    Alternate-nostril breath Nadi Shodhana
    Balances the nervous system and the mind. 5 min.
  3. 3
    Cat–Cow Marjaryasana–Bitilasana
    Warm the spine with breath — 8 slow rounds.
  4. 4
    Child's pose Balasana
    Forehead down, soften the back. 1 min.
  5. 5
    Reclined twist Supta Matsyendrasana
    Both sides, releasing the lower back. 1 min each.
  6. 6
    Rest Savasana
    Lie still, let the practice settle. 5 min.
Today's affirmation
I meet this change with curiosity, not fear. My body is wise.
02
Day Two · Move

Gentle Flow & Breath

Understand

Movement matters more now than ever. A weight-bearing flow protects your bones, builds the muscle that naturally slips away with falling oestrogen, and moves stagnant energy so mood and circulation lift. But this is not about pushing — it is gentle and breath-led. Each breath leads each movement.

Move slowly. Let the inhale lengthen you and the exhale soften you. If you feel heat rising, slow the pace and lengthen your out-breath. Use a block under your hands in standing poses, and keep a wall nearby for balance. Today's flow runs a full 45–50 minutes — give yourself the whole arc, from warm-up to deep rest.

A note on breath

Pair the movement with steady Ujjayi (ocean) breath — a soft hush at the back of the throat, in and out through the nose. It warms the body, holds your focus, and turns the flow into a moving meditation.

Full flow≈ 45–50 min
  1. 1
    Warm-up
    Seated breath, neck & shoulder rolls, Cat–Cow, thread-the-needle. ≈ 8 min.
  2. 2
    Gentle Sun Salutations Surya Namaskar
    4 slow rounds, one breath per movement. ≈ 10 min.
  3. 3
    Warrior II Virabhadrasana II
    Strong legs, open chest. Both sides, 5 breaths.
  4. 4
    Triangle Trikonasana
    Lengthen the side body; block under the hand if needed.
  5. 5
    Tree Vrksasana
    Balance builds bone. Wall nearby, both sides.
  6. 6
    Goddess Utkata Konasana
    Wide squat, breathe into the heat. 5 breaths.
  7. 7
    Bridge Setu Bandhasana
    Lift hips on the inhale. 3 rounds.
  8. 8
    Cobra Bhujangasana
    Gentle backbend, elbows soft.
  9. 9
    Pigeon & seated forward fold
    Open hips, then fold and release. Both sides.
  10. 10
    Legs-up-the-wall & rest Viparita Karani · Savasana
    Bhramari breath, then stillness. ≈ 8 min.
Today's affirmation
With every breath I create space. I am strong and supple.

Day Three · Nourish

Food as Medicine

Understand

What you eat can genuinely soften this transition — and it need not be expensive or imported. The best foods for perimenopause are often already in your kitchen.

  • Gentle plant oestrogens — flaxseed (alsi), sesame (til), soy, chickpeas and lentils softly echo the body's own oestrogen.
  • For bones — ragi (finger millet), sesame, curd/yoghurt, drumstick (moringa) leaves and dark leafy greens are rich in calcium.
  • For mood & sleep — pumpkin seeds, bananas, nuts and greens carry magnesium.
  • To cool the heat — papaya, pomegranate, melon, cucumber and coconut water ease hot flushes.
  • To calm inflammation — turmeric, ginger and garlic; omega-3 from walnuts, flaxseed and fish.
  • Protect muscle — include some protein at every meal: dal, beans, curd, eggs, paneer, nuts.

Gently ease off the common flush-triggers: excess caffeine, alcohol, refined sugar and very spicy food. The simple rule — eat a rainbow, eat whole, eat at regular times.

Digestive & cooling practice≈ 25 min
  1. 1
    Seated Cat–Cow
    Wake the belly and spine. 8 rounds.
  2. 2
    Wind-relieving pose Pavanamuktasana
    Hug knees to belly, rock gently. Aids digestion.
  3. 3
    Seated twist Ardha Matsyendrasana
    Massages the organs. Both sides, 5 breaths.
  4. 4
    Bridge Setu Bandhasana
    Gentle lift, 3 rounds.
  5. 5
    Reclined bound angle Supta Baddha Konasana
    Soles together, breathe into the belly. 3 min.
  6. 6
    Cooling breath Sheetali / Sheetkari
    Inhale across the tongue — wonderfully cooling. 5 min.
  7. 7
    Rest Savasana
    2–3 min of stillness.
Today's affirmation
I nourish my body with kindness. What I eat is an act of self-love.
04
Day Four · Soothe

Mind, Mood & Affirmation

Understand

Fluctuating hormones reach the brain too — irritability, anxiety, sudden tears and brain fog are real, common, and not your fault. A few small, steady lifestyle shifts give the mind firmer ground:

  • Move a little every day — even a 20-minute walk lifts mood.
  • Step into morning sunlight — for vitamin D, mood and your sleep rhythm.
  • Drink enough water; eat at regular times.
  • Protect your rest — gentler mornings, kinder boundaries.
  • Stay connected — talk, share, lean on others. You are not alone in this.
  • Ease back evening caffeine, alcohol and screens.
On affirmations

The mind believes what it hears most often. Spoken daily, affirmations gently rewrite the inner story — from "something is wrong with me" to "I am changing, and I am whole." Pair them with slow breath so they land in the body, not just the head. Place a hand on your heart, breathe, and say the words as if to a dear friend — because you are.

Calming practice≈ 25 min
  1. 1
    Humming-bee breath Bhramari
    Soft hum on the exhale — calms anxiety quickly. 5 min.
  2. 2
    Seated forward fold Paschimottanasana
    Fold gently, knees soft. Turns the gaze inward.
  3. 3
    Supported Child's pose Balasana
    Rest on a cushion or bolster. 2 min.
  4. 4
    Legs-up-the-wall Viparita Karani
    Quiets the mind, eases tired legs. 4 min.
  5. 5
    Affirmation in stillness Savasana
    Hand on heart, repeat today's words on each exhale. 5 min.
Today's affirmation
I am not losing myself — I am becoming more myself.

Day Five · Rest

Breath for Deep Sleep

Understand

Sleep is often the first thing perimenopause disturbs — night sweats, a racing mind, the 3 a.m. waking. Your breath is the most powerful, free sleep tool you own. Long, slow exhalations switch on the body's "rest-and-digest" mode, slow the heart, cool the body and quiet the thoughts.

And it works both ways: breathing well through the day changes how you feel — calmer mood, clearer head, steadier energy — and how deeply you sleep at night. A good day and a good night feed each other.

  • Keep the same sleep and wake time, even on weekends.
  • A cool, dark, screen-free room; light, airy nightwear.
  • No caffeine after noon; a lighter, earlier dinner.
  • A wind-down ritual — dim the lights, a warm shower, this breath practice.
  • If you wake in the night, breathe long exhales instead of reaching for the phone.
Evening wind-down flow15 min
  1. 1
    Legs-up-the-wall Viparita Karani
    Settle, soften, breathe low. 4 min.
  2. 2
    Reclined bound angle Supta Baddha Konasana
    Belly breath, heart open. 3 min.
  3. 3
    Reclined twist Supta Matsyendrasana
    Wring out the day. Both sides, 1 min each.
  4. 4
    Humming-bee breath Bhramari
    Soft hum, lengthening exhale. 3 min.
  5. 5
    Extended-exhale breath 4–7–8
    In for 4, hold 7, out for 8 — already in bed. ≈ 2 min.
Today's affirmation
I release the day. My body knows how to rest.

Day Six · Strengthen

Strength & Stability

Understand

As oestrogen falls, bones thin and muscle is lost more easily — so this is the season to build strength, not shrink from it. Weight-bearing standing poses and balance work protect your bones, joints and independence for decades to come. Strong women age well.

Hold the poses a few breaths longer than feels easy — that gentle effort is what signals your bones and muscles to stay strong. Keep a wall or chair close for balance, and let your breath stay steady even as the legs work. Strength here is kindness to your future self.

Strength practice≈ 35 min
  1. 1
    Warm-up
    Sun salutations, 3 slow rounds. ≈ 6 min.
  2. 2
    Chair Utkatasana
    Sit into the heels, arms rise. Hold 5 breaths.
  3. 3
    Warrior I & II Virabhadrasana I & II
    Build heat and leg strength. Both sides.
  4. 4
    Goddess holds Utkata Konasana
    Wide squat, hold and breathe. 5 breaths.
  5. 5
    Tree & standing balance Vrksasana
    Balance for bone strength. Wall nearby.
  6. 6
    Plank & Bird-dog
    Knees down as needed — core & back. Hold steady.
  7. 7
    Locust Salabhasana
    Strengthens the whole back line. 3 rounds.
  8. 8
    Bridge, then rest Setu Bandhasana · Savasana
    Cool down with Nadi Shodhana. ≈ 6 min.
Today's affirmation
I am strong. I am steady. I stand firmly in this stage of my life.

Day Seven · Carry forward

Your Daily Ritual

Understand

A short practice done daily beats a long one done rarely. From everything you have tasted this week, keep a simple 10–15 minute ritual that leaves you relaxed, lifts your mood, and helps you wake up refreshed. Do it most mornings, or each evening — whichever your life allows.

Some days you will move through all of it; some days just three slow breaths. Both count. Consistency, not perfection, is what carries you gently through the years ahead.

This is not the ending of your vitality — it is a doorway. Move with intention, eat with kindness, breathe through the heat, rest without guilt, and speak to yourself softly. You have everything you need.

Keep-forever ritual≈ 10–15 min
  1. 1
    Belly breathing
    Arrive, settle the nervous system. 3 min.
  2. 2
    Cat–Cow & a few Sun Salutations
    Wake the spine, warm the body.
  3. 3
    One balance pose Tree
    A moment of focus and bone strength.
  4. 4
    One hip-opener or forward fold
    Release where you hold tension.
  5. 5
    Breath Nadi Shodhana or Bhramari
    2 min to balance and calm.
  6. 6
    Stillness & one affirmation
    Choose the line that speaks to you today. 2 min.
Today's affirmation
Every day, in every breath, I return home to myself.
